Why Is It Taking So Long?

October 25, 2018

An Anamorphic lens is what is called a cylindrical lens, which means the glass is in a cylindrical shape. This is different from traditional lenses which are spherical in shape. Lots of people produce spherical lenses, so cutting, polishing, and coating small spherical lenses in large volume isn’t a problem.

In making our Anamorphic lens we are using the same lens supplier who has produced all our lenses. In making our existing lenses our supplier relies on a network of glass makers who cut, polish, and coat the glass. Unfortunately, that network of glass makers aren’t available for the Anamorphic because they only produce spherical lenses.

Therefore we had to develop a new set of glass providers for this lens. We spent weeks before Kickstarter, (yes over 9 months ago), meeting, sampling, and testing several potential providers. We selected a handful who could produce the sample qualities we were looking for as well as a reliable production schedule.

A very long story short, producing this lens has been much harder than any of the glass providers estimated. The yield rates are low, which means for every say 1K they try to produce only about 100-150 are coming out the other end at the quality we require. Therefore every schedule, including our worst case scenario, has been missed.

Thankfully our partners continue to push forward as they sort out how to produce enough glass at the quality we require. In addition we have people on the ground and are sampling additional glass provides in order to increase our production quantities.
